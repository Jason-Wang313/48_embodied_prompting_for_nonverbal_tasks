# Hostile Reviewer Response

The strongest criticism is correct: the benchmark is synthetic and assumes upstream cue detection and binding estimates. The final manuscript treats that as scope, not as hidden evidence.

Response:

- The paper contributes a conservative action-contract interface for nonverbal prompts.
- The full-scale suite tests 18 cue families, 6 domains, 5 embodiments, 7 binding regimes, 7 policies, 4 ambiguity regimes, and 2 safety modes.
- The proposed method is not defended as deployed gesture perception or certified safety.
- The positive claim is that conservative graph binding improves utility and unsafe-execution behavior over captioning, generic multimodal prompting, affordance filtering, and unconservative graph execution.
- The paper reports safety-cue partial miss and cross-user style shift as boundary cases.
